# About BexleyMesh

BexleyMesh is a community-driven project dedicated to building a resilient, off-grid mesh communication network in the Bexley, Ohio area. Using low-power, long-range LoRa radios running the MeshCore firmware, we enable text communication and GPS location sharing without reliance on cellular towers or the internet.

## Our Goals
 1. Create a network that provides a reliable connection to the mesh network outdoor and indoors for every corner of Bexley.
 2. Establish repeater locations outside of Bexley that allow the Bexley community to connect to the broader Central Ohio mesh network.
 3. Provide learning opportunities and materials for makers and students interested in STEM subjects to learn about electronics and RF communication. 

## What We Do

 - **Deploy Repeaters** - We install solar-powered repeater nodes at strategic locations throughout the Bexley area to extend network coverage and reliability.
 - **Support New Members** - We help community members get set up with compatible hardware and walk them through the process of joining the network.
 - **Educate & Share** - We publish guides, tutorials, and project updates to help others learn about mesh networking technology.
 - **Collaborate** - We work alongside the broader Central Ohio Mesh community and the MeshCore open-source project to improve the tools we use to keep the network functional, reliable, and free.

## How It Works

Our network is built on [MeshCore](https://meshcore.co.uk) firmware running on affordable LoRa radio hardware.  Mesh networks, like Meshcore rely on a segment of the RF spectrum that the FCC has opened for free use without the need for a license or approval.  Meshcore takes advantage of this portion of the spectrum to allow simple text communication without the need for internet or cellular coverage.  Meshcore devices can communicate directly device to device, but the real benefit comes when we integrate small, solar powered repeaters into the community.  These small repeaters enable communication across a broader area.

There are three types of devices deployed on the network:
  - **Repeater nodes** are fixed installations (preferably solar-powered) that relay messages across the network. 
  - **Companion nodes** are handheld devices carried by community members. Together, they form a communication grid that covers the local area.
  - **Room nodes** are devices that typically remain stationary and provide a storage of messsages that can be retreived when when a user reconnects to the network.

Private messages are encrytped end-to-end, ensuring privacy and security.  In addition to private direct messages, the network also allows for the creation of private Channels and Rooms.  Channels are like group messages that are also encrypted like privates messages, but the messages are transitory.  Meaning that if you are not on the network at the time the message was sent, you will not recieve the message once you return.  Rooms rely on devices that remain on the network to receive the message, allowing you to retreive the message upon you reconnection to the network.

## Get Involved

There are many ways to participate:

 1. **Get a Radio** - Pick up a compatible LoRa device and join the network. See our [Getting Started](page.html?page=getting-started) guide.
 2. **Host a Repeater** - If you have a good location (rooftop, high elevation), consider hosting a repeater node.
 3. **Join the Community** - Connect with us on [Discord](https://discord.com/channels/1280671076644425749/1280693593002082436) to ask questions and share your experiences.
 4. **Contribute** - This is an open-source project. Visit our [GitHub](https://github.com/DavidJayMartin/BexleyMesh) to contribute code, documentation, or ideas.
